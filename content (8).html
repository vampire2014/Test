Focus on NAEP is a publication series developed to highlight findings and summarize information on the ongoing development and implementation of the National Assessment of Educational Progress (NAEP). This issue details the participation and performance of public school students who are English language learners (ELL) on the NAEP reading and mathematics assessments.

It is important for NAEP to assess as many students selected as possible to reflect the educational performance of all students accurately. Accordingly, NAEP took measures to ensure that English language learners were represented in its assessments. Prior to 1996, NAEP excluded non-native English speakers who had not been enrolled for at least two years in an English-speaking school and students that were determined by their school to be unable to successfully complete the assessment because of their limited English language proficiency. This resulted in NAEP not being able to adequately reflect what ELL students know and can do. The provision of accommodations for ELL students, beginning in 1996 in mathematics and 1998 in reading, expanded the participation of non-native English speakers, resulting in a greater percentage of ELL students participating in NAEP. Currently, the policy states that if an ELL student has been in the country for more than one academic school year, they should participate in NAEP. For more information on the policy, click here: https://www.nagb.org/content/nagb/assets/documents/policies/naep_testandreport_studentswithdisabilities.pdf. These changes in NAEP policy have changed the picture of the ELL population and their performance on NAEP assessments.
